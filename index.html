Major period
A roughly two-hour window centered on the moon being overhead or underfoot. It is the stronger solunar influence, especially when it overlaps dawn, dusk or a weather change.
Minor period
A roughly one-hour window around moonrise or moonset. It is a smaller possible movement boost. Neither period overrides wind, food, bedding, pressure or hunting pressure.

ZIP-code locations are provided by Zippopotam.us. Weather comes from Open-Meteo. Astronomy is calculated with SunCalc. Solunar windows are approximate planning aids, not guarantees of deer movement. Wind direction, access route, pressure, food, temperature changes and trail-camera observations should carry more weight. Tennessee general big-game hours are generally 30 minutes before legal sunrise through 30 minutes after legal sunset; always check current TWRA and WMA-specific rules.

Major vs. minor
Major is the stronger two-hour overhead/underfoot window. Minor is the shorter moonrise/moonset window. Both are possible boosts, not guarantees.
